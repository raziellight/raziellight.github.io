Where did we go wrong?

I was confronted with this meme one day.

It may be a little demeaning, but It got me thinking, what happened? Where did we go wrong?

Ultimately all we need for survival is oxygen, water, food, shelter, and defense. Yet in today’s age we grow up and go to school. We learn mathematics, english, science, and social studies, yet they fail to teach us how to just simply survive. They completely skip basic survival skills. At 18 we are tossed out into the world or into higher education where we are in the rat race to provide value to society. Providing value determines if we are successful and well off or if we survive off food stamps and food banks. You spend your entire life surviving. Twelve to twenty four years of education plus a demanding job for the rest of your life all to just take care of oxygen, water, food, shelter, and defense. What happened? Where did we go wrong?

At the heart of the challenge is money. We all need money to survive. You can think of money like a lock and a key. The money is flows into the company from customers and then further flows through to the next company and to the next. I don’t know if you’ve ever tried to think of a way to provide free goods to people. It is exceptionally hard because you think about providing something for free, but you will constantly ask yourself where you will get the money to pay for it. Money is like a virus. If one person charges for something that demands the customers to head off to charge for their own goods to get money to unlock the original product they were seeking. In this way money spreads like wildfire. It’s all free or none of it is free. Charging for goods demands you go off and do your own thing to get your own money despite it may not be having anything to do with survival. This is why innovation is so big. This is also why we have every imaginable variation of salad dressing on the store shelves. Everyone is trying to get their piece of the pie.

But shouldn’t survival come first? Shouldn’t we have access to basic clean food and shelter without the demand to provide some innovative value? That means survival before innovation. What if we were taught in school not just mental acrobatics but simply they means to survival before else and to provide that freely to everyone on a community level. People spend their entire lives developing to provide something unique to a world where there is very little room to be unique. It is spectacular how much variation we have developed for ourselves as it is.

Ultimately the system failed when there lacked easy access to clean energy, communication, short mobility, water, food, sanitation, and suitable mates. All while having a low impact on the environment and health outcomes. Everything else is a distraction. Now imagine there was a way on a community level to provide access to these things with as little fuss as possible. What would we need to have? Well you would need homes for the people. You would need communication and energy production throughout it all. You would need some way to farm food, and you would need a town hall to socialize. And you would need sanitation and garbage disposal. We have all this now, but rent costs 0.6K-1.5K a month, food costs 300$ a month, nothing else is free.

We are living in a technological revolution. They are now printing 3d printed houses for $4000. Tesla is talking about a 10 to 15k homes. Vertical farming is taking off everywhere. We continue to get upgrades to our communication networks. Computers now exist with crypto that allows decentralized autonomous organizations (DAO) Solar is now priced ready to compete with traditional forms of energy. Yet there are still incentives to exclude the people from owning it. To make it commercial. This challenges regular families to still provide some value to society that is innovative rather than survival based. Because they are not owners to these goods.

Now imagine if there was a community that was based on survival, not innovation. That bought into the idea of survival first and freedom over the restraint of paywalls. I’m talking about buying into a community that is free. Enter pockets of freedom. Imagine a small community, anywhere from 20 to 1500 to 16000 people small. They would own their own vertical farm and inexpensive housing. There would be a town hall and everyone would be producing energy from solar. The key point is that everything inside this community would be free. It would be like a self contained pocket of freedom. It would produce excess to sell off to the broader economy, but in itself it would produce everything it needs to survive.

  • Town hall for socializing and more efficient education
  • Vertical farm with excess to sell
  • 10k Homes with airbnb potential
  • Communication and energy grid with potential to sell back to the economy
  • Everything is close by in a 1km radius. Scooter accessible
  • Automation to reduce labor
  • DAO to coordinate everyone

And if you think about it we could easily transform current communities. Unfortunately it is still costly to rent, so this may not be ideal. But affordable housing may drive costs down. You could easily imagine having a vertical farm where an apartment complex would typically be. And with community approval turn the high schools into town halls. Education would be more efficient so there would be less need for schools because there would be no need to educate for such extreme lengths of time.

People would essentially invest in their freedom for much less than it would cost now. This would allow them to gain economic freedom without having to go through the typical pain of providing innovative value to survive. Each pocket of freedom would pay a tax to a DAO that oversees the forming of pockets of freedom. This would allow people to leverage off the current infrastructure to essentially provide near free access to anyone who is part of the DAO. As much as i hate lotteries if you can not afford to invest yourself you may enter a lottery for less to play your luck. This lottery wouldn’t be a hit and miss thing, however. Signing up for the lottery would be a no man left behind. It would only be a matter of time until you won the lottery. It would be a no man or woman left behind lottery. I would hope the government would recognize the money it would save by affording homeless in the pockets of freedom and grant the investment it would take. However, we can’t count on the government since they have let us down time and time again. The government is a failure. You could always take out a loan from a traditional bank, and buy your freedom for much less work than paying rent in a traditional community.

There you have it. If there was some independent community where it was self sustaining run by a DAO there would be no need for money. It could produce excess and provide to the economy thereby taking advantage of innovations while providing for the basic survival needs.

Where to go from here?
